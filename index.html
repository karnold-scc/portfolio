Component Two - Presentation

The second course component was to create an effective slideshow. Although this component was not presented verbally, the objective was to create a slideshow that would be engaging and informative without overloading the viewer with words on screen.

For my slideshow, I chose to discuss Computer Forensics. I had slides touching on topics such as a definition of Computer Forensics, its use cases, and some tools and techniques associated with the subject. I also included some relevant crimes and cases where Computer Forensics helped to solve the crime and lead to a conviction.

Component Three - Elevator Pitch

The third component was what is known as an 'elevator pitch'. The idea is that you are riding in an elevator with the CEO or hiring manager of a company, and you only have that approximately 30 second elevator ride to convince that person to hire you.

Link Here

Components Four and Five - Mock Interview and Guest Speakers

These components required no submission, save for some exit cards for our wonderful guest speakers. We had four guest speaker activities, including some exciting guests who are involved with our local tech landscape. The mock interviews were conducted on site at the college, and were a great way to simulate a real interview in a low-stakes environment. Post-interview, we were given some excellent feedback in order to improve our real-life interview perfomance.

Component 6 - Presentation

Our final component, other than this portfolio page, was a verbal Presentation in front of our classmates. I chose to discuss some basic data types and structures used in programming. This was a good opportunity to practice some presentation skills, as well as discuss a topic that may have been unfamiliar to many of my classmates.
